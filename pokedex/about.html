Creator

The Pokémon franchise began in 1996 in Japan, created by Satoshi Tajiri and Ken Sugimori. Inspired by Tajiri’s childhood hobby of collecting insects, the idea evolved into a game where players would capture and trade creatures called Pokémon. The first games, Pokémon Red and Green for the Game Boy, quickly became a national phenomenon and later spread worldwide. As the franchise grew, it expanded into an animated TV series starring Ash Ketchum, a trading card game, movies, toys, and countless spin-off games. Today, Pokémon is one of the most successful entertainment franchises in history, known for its iconic characters, ongoing game releases, and global community.
